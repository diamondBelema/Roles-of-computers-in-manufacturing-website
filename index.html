Roles of Computer in Manufacturing

Group 11 - PAMO University of Medical Sciences

Lecturer: Dr. Anireh

Introduction

Computers have transformed manufacturing by enhancing efficiency, precision, and automation. From Computer-Aided Manufacturing (CAM) to AI-driven production, they reduce human error and optimize processes.

Initially used for calculations and control systems, computers now enable automation, precision cutting, and faster production. CAD (Computer-Aided Design) improves product modeling, while CAM enhances machine operations.

Ancient vs Modern Manufacturing

Ancient: Manufacturing relied on manual labor, handmade tools, and simple techniques. Production was slow, inconsistent, and prone to errors.

Modern: Automation, CAD/CAM software, and robotics ensure high precision, faster production, and improved quality. The Internet of Things (IoT) enables real-time monitoring, and AI enhances decision-making.

Roles of Computer in Manufacturing

1. Supply Chain Management (SCM)

Technologies like AI, machine learning (ML), and IoT optimize demand forecasting, inventory management, and logistics.

2. Design and Prototyping

Digital prototyping allows engineers to test and modify designs in a virtual environment, ensuring faster iterations and better collaboration.

3. Production Automation

Automated machines use AI and software controls to improve efficiency, consistency, and speed in production lines.

4. Quality Control

AI-powered image recognition and machine learning identify defects in real time, reducing product errors.

5. Data Analytics & AI

Big data helps manufacturers analyze performance trends and optimize processes for better efficiency.

6. Robotics in Manufacturing

Industrial robots handle repetitive tasks with high precision, improving speed and consistency.

7. Internet of Things (IoT)

Connected devices in factories enable predictive maintenance and real-time monitoring of production.

8. Challenges & Limitations

Despite benefits, challenges like cybersecurity risks, high initial costs, and reliance on skilled labor exist.

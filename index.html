Roles of Computer in Manufacturing

Group 11, PAMO University of Medical Sciences

Lecturer in Charge: Dr. Anireh

Introduction

Computers have transformed manufacturing by enhancing efficiency, precision, and automation. From Computer-Aided Manufacturing (CAM) to AI-driven production, they reduce human error and optimize processes...

Ancient vs Modern Manufacturing

Ancient: Manual labor, handmade tools, slow production.

Modern: Automation, CAD/CAM, robotics, IoT, AI-driven efficiency.

Roles of Computers in Manufacturing

1. Supply Chain Management (SCM)

AI, IoT, and analytics enhance demand forecasting, inventory, and logistics.

2. Design and Prototyping

Digital prototyping enables engineers to test and modify designs in a virtual environment.

3. Computer Manufacturing Automation

Computers control machines, enabling automated processes and precision manufacturing.

4. Production Planning and Control

Manages human resources, raw materials, and machinery for efficient production.

5. Quality Control and Inspection

Ensures products meet quality standards through rigorous inspection processes.

Limitations of Computers in Manufacturing

1. Cybersecurity Risks

Cyber-attacks can disrupt production and leak sensitive data.

2. High Maintenance Costs

Frequent updates and repairs add to operational costs.

3. Need for Skilled Workforce

Regular upskilling is required to keep up with technological advancements.

Conclusion

Computers have revolutionized manufacturing, increasing efficiency and competitiveness. However, challenges like cybersecurity, costs, and workforce adaptation must be addressed. AI-driven smart factories will shape the future.
